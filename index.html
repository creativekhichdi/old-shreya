Our Mission

To be amongst the top 25 Pharmaceutical companies of India by the year 2021-22 and to achieve this objective through both organic and inorganic growth.

Our Vision

To be a globally admired Pharmaceutical company for world-class quality and innovation.

Shreya Life Sciences is a state-of-the-art pharmaceutical organisation involved in research, manufacturing and distribution of medicinal formulations and products.

Beginning its journey in 1995 in Russia, Shreya Life Sciences started as a pharmaceutical distributing and marketing company. In the course of over two decades, Shreya Life Sciences has pioneered innovations and achieved milestones in commissioning new product lines & formulations, clinical studies as well as contract manufacturing. It now boasts of a global presence with operations in Russia, CIS countries, South East Asia, Africa, and Latin America.

Proprietary Technologies
Hynidase

A spreading factor that enhances the effect of local anaesthesia, it has wide clinical applications, especially cataract surgery. The source of our Hynidase is ovine compared to bovine originating products in the market, thus being free of TSE/BSE infections.

Insulin Formulations

We have a dedicated Insulin Formulation Facility that formulates both 40 IU & 100 IU insulin. Our insulin products are created in regular, NPH, 30/70 and 50/50 varieties.

Dextrans & Iron Dextrans

We provide in-house formulations of Dextrans and Iron Dextrans, which are manufactured as APIs.

Salmon Calcitonins

Formulations of Salmon Calcitonins are created in the forms of injections and nasal spray for treating osteoporosis.

Research Projects
Phase III Trial & Study

We conducted Phase III studies to launch our new Insulin Formulations to establish its credibility and effectivity at par with products available in Indian markets. As part of the regulatory requirements for Product Registration in India, the data was submitted to DCGI, India.

Phase III Trial & Study

For Generex Biotechnology’s Buccal Delivery of Insulin Formulation, we conducted a large mutil-centric Phase III studies on 198 patients. The study involved the effectiveness of Buccal Delivery as non-inferior compared to traditional insulin injections. The findings proved that Buccal Delivery is equally effective and well accepted by Type 2 Diabetes patients.
